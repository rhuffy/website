Team Structure

The team is divided into two groups of subsystems, mechanical and electrical. Our 50 members may be working/living/contributing in any of these subteams.

  • group4 executive positions

    Captain, Chief Engineer, EE Lead, Vehicle Architect
    The EE lead manages the two electrical teams of low voltage systems and software, while the chief engineer takes care of the mechanical systems, which include battery, powertrain, and aerodynamics. These two larger roles, and the third role of vehicle architect, are in turn managed by the captain. On the nontechnical side, the captain also directs a business team and a publicity team.

  • settings13 mechanical subteams

    A Arms, Arbs & Rockers, Battery, Enclosures, Brakes, Aero, Pedal Box, Powertrain, R&D, Suspension, Wheel Packages, Testing, Cooling
    Each subteam is lead by one member elected at the end of the previous model year. They and the rest of the subteam is responsible for designing and manufacturing their part of the car. Each subteam works independently but together they all work together to achieve a rolling chassis.

  • memory2 electrical subteams

    Electrical engineering, Software
    Members of these subteams build all the electronics and software necessary for the car to accelerate and drive during the competition. They divide up labor corresponding to seperate projects and report back to the EE lead.

  • assignment2 administrative divisions

    Business, Publicity
    The Business division’s main role is to prepare for the Cost event at competition. Therefore they manage all purchases made by the team and donations made to the team. The Publicity division maintains the outward appearance of the team. They manage the website and social media of the team.
