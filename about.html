About

MIT Motorsports Mission Statement

MIT Motorsports strives to provide students with the best means to learn about the engineering process by emphasizing rigorous engineering, strong technical expertise, and effective management.

Formula SAE

The Formula SAE competition tasks students to design, build, and engineer a vehicle that fulfills the requirements of a potential customer- an amateur driving enthusiast. Starting from the ground and building up, each team optimizes their own design to compete against an international group of universities where they are judged on the cost, business case, and design itself before hitting the track for a variety of dynamic driving events.
Formula SAE pushes students to apply what they have learned in the classroom to a real-world project. Having the opportunity to oversee and complete the design of both specific parts and the overall systems provides team members an unparalleled experience of seeing a high-level project to completion. With applications ranging from the automotive, aerospace, consumer electronics, and data acquisition, students can prepare themselves to engineer where they’re needed most.

Engineering Our Future

MIT Motorsports recognizes that alternative propulsion methods are vital for a cleaner future, which led us to convert to an all electric powertrain in 2013. We are motivated to help improve electric vehicle technology to reach a future where electric vehicles are a practical alternative to gasoline vehicles.

The switch to electric power does not mean that performance is sacrificed in the slightest. At the heart of the team is still the desire for speed , so we aim to develop an electric car that exceeds the handling, efficiency, and reliability of a gasoline-powered counterpart.

Community Outreach

In addition to rolling out a high-performing car, we are also committed to education and promotion of science and engineering in our local community. The team supports and attends events such as the Cambridge Science Festival, Drive EV Day, and the MIT Open House, and regularly gives shop tours and demonstrations to elementary and high school students from the area.

On campus, we are involved with the Edgerton Center Outreach Program and the MIT Museum. The team also collaborates with local businesses to support grassroots programs and community involvement. In the summer months, we work with summer programs such as the Women’s Technology Program to inspire younger students to explore the many possibilities within the STEM field.
